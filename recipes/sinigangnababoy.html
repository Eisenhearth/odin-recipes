Sinigang na Baboy

Description

Sinigang na Baboy is the ultimate Filipino comfort food, known for its signature sour broth and tender pork. It’s perfect for a rainy day or a hearty family meal.

Ingredients

  1. 500g - 1kg Pork belly (liempo) or spare ribs, cut into cubes
  2. 1.5 - 2 Liters Water
  3. 1 medium Red onion, quartered
  4. 2-3 medium Tomatoes, quartered
  5. 1 pack (20g-40g) Tamarind (Sinigang) mix, or fresh tamarind juice to taste
  6. 1 medium Radish (labanos)
  7. 1 bunch String beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  8. 2 pieces Eggplant (talong), sliced diagonally
  9. 1-2 pieces Green chili (siling haba)
  10. 1 bunch Water spinach (kangkong) or Pechay
  11. 2 tbsp Fish sauce (patis) or salt to taste

Cooking Guide

  • Sear and Boil: In a large pot, add the pork and water (or rice wash). Bring to a boil and skim off any scum that rises to the top for a clear broth.
  • Aromatics: Add the onions and tomatoes. Lower the heat to a simmer and cover. Cook until the pork is tender (usually 30-45 minutes depending on the cut).
  • Sour and Season: Pour in the tamarind mix. Stir well and add the fish sauce. Adjust the sourness and saltiness according to your preference.
  • Add Hardy Veggies: Add the radish and green chilies. Let it simmer for about 2-3 minutes.
  • Add Remaining Veggies: Add the string beans and eggplant. Cook for another 3 minutes until they are slightly tender but still have a bite.
  • The Greens: Lastly, add the kangkong or pechay. Turn off the heat and cover the pot; the residual heat will perfectly wilt the greens without overcooking them.
