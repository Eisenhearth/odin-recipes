Calamares

Description

Calamares is a beloved Filipino appetizer and street food made from deep-fried battered squid rings.
The secret to great calamares is a light, crispy coating and tender, non-rubbery squid.

Ingredients

  • 500g Fresh squid, cleaned and sliced into 1/2-inch rings
  • 1 tbsp Calamansi juice or lemon juice (to remove the "fishy" scent)
  • 1/2 tsp Salt and black pepper
  • 1 cup All-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup Cornstarch (this makes it extra crispy)
  • 1 tsp Garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp Paprika (optional, for color)
  • 1 large Egg, beaten
  • 1/2 cup Cold water or chilled seltzer water (for a lighter batter)
  • 2 cups Cooking oil (vegetable or canola)

Cooking Guide

  • Prep the Squid: After slicing the squid into rings, pat them extremely dry with paper towels. Excess moisture is the enemy of a crispy coating. Toss the rings in calamansi juice, salt, and pepper. Let them sit for 10 minutes.
  • The Flour Dredge: In a bowl, mix the flour, cornstarch, garlic powder, and paprika. Take the dried squid rings and coat them lightly in this dry mixture. Shake off any excess.
  • The Batter: In a separate bowl, whisk the egg and cold water together. Dip the floured squid rings into the egg mixture, then dredge them back into the flour mixture once more for a double coating.
  • Heat the Oil: Heat the oil in a deep pan to about 175°C (350°F). If you don't have a thermometer, drop a small piece of batter in; if it sizzles and rises immediately, it’s ready.
  • Quick Fry: Fry the squid rings in batches. Do not overcrowd the pan. Fry for only 1 to 2 minutes until they turn a light golden brown.
  • Drain: Use a slotted spoon to remove the calamares and place them on a wire rack or paper towels to drain excess oil.
