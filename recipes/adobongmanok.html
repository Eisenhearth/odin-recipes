Adobong Manok

Description

Adobong Manok is widely considered the unofficial national dish of the Philippines.
It’s a masterclass in pantry-staple cooking, utilizing a vinegar and soy sauce braise to create tender, savory chicken with a signature tangy kick.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg Chicken pieces
  • 1/2 cup Soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup White cane vinegar
  • 1 head Garlic
  • 1 tsp Whole black peppercorns
  • 3-4 pieces Dried bay leaves
  • 2 tbsp Cooking oil
  • 1 cup Water
  • 1 tbsp Brown sugar
  • Salt

Cooking Guide

  1. Marinate (Optional but Recommended): In a large bowl, combine the chicken, soy sauce, garlic, and peppercorns. Let it sit for at least 30 minutes, or up to overnight in the fridge for maximum flavor.
  2. Sear the Chicken: Heat oil in a wide pan or pot over medium-high heat. Remove the chicken from the marinade (save the liquid!) and sear the pieces until the skin is browned and slightly crispy. Work in batches if needed.
  3. Simmer: Pour the remaining marinade and the bay leaves into the pan with the chicken. Add water if you prefer more sauce. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat to a simmer. Cover and cook for 20-25 minutes until the chicken is tender.
  4. Add Vinegar: Pour in the vinegar. Important: Do not stir for the first 2-3 minutes after adding vinegar to allow the "raw" acidity to cook off.
  5. Balance & Reduce: Stir in the sugar (if using). If you like a thick, oily sauce (Adobong Tuyo), remove the lid and continue simmering until the liquid reduces and the chicken starts to fry in its own fat.
  6. Serve: Transfer to a serving dish and pour the remaining sauce over the top. It's best served with a generous amount of steamed white rice.
